DDH

The Diamond Discovery Hub (DDH) is one of the services of the European Diamond Capacity Hub (EDCH). The Diamond Discovery Hub (DDH) aims to become a comprehensive registry for institutionally published and scholar-led Open Access journals without author fees in Europe, designed to improve the visibility and discoverability of diamond OA journals.

Bibliodiversity support The DDH indexes exclusively verified Diamond Open Access Journals, validated against a set of transparent criteria including, but not limited to, the absence of fees for the author and the reader.

The DDH Diamond criteria are aligned with the Diamond Open Access Standard, and both the criteria and the standard have been established with the Diamond Open Access community.



It has no limitation in terms of disciplines or languages.



It is currently limited to the European Research Area (ERA) and the European Research Area and Innovation Committee (ERAC), which is broader than the European Union or the European continent.

INCLUSION PROCESS

Element Description
Joining options The DDH selects its trusted sources through a two-step process, described here: https://ddh.diamas.org/docs/become-a-trusted-source/.



The first step requires to be registered on the EDCH registry: https://registry.diamas.org/register. The Registry recognizes and accepts various types of actors: Institutional Publishers, Publishing Service Providers, Service, Tools and Technology providers, as well as National Capacity Centers.



The second step consists in the validation of the registered organization as a DDH trusted source. The DDH team makes a few additional verifications about the registered organization (verification steps documentation in progress).



Once validated, the Trusted source has to accept the DDH Terms of service and take full responsibility to check the compliance of their journal with the DDH six Diamond criteria.

The DDH does not check afterwards the accuracy of the information sent by the Trusted sources, as it fully relies on them for this work.



Here are the useful links for the Trusted sources:

- Terms of service: https://ddh.diamas.org/docs/terms-of-service/

- Diamond criteria: https://ddh.diamas.org/docs/diamond-journals/

- Guidelines for the criteria's validation: https://ddh.diamas.org/docs/diamond-open-access-journal-verification-guidelines/

- FAQ about criteria's valdation: https://ddh.diamas.org/docs/faq/
Data collection process The data collection can happen through various options:

- upload of an Excel spreadsheet (based on the JMEF format)

- filling of an on-line form (for individual journals)

- upload of JMEF format through an OJS plugin

- harvesting through OAI-PMH of a JMEF dedicated set



The various options for data ingestion are described here: https://ddh.diamas.org/docs/data-ingestion/.
Application form for providers An application form is available upon registration, see: https://ddh.diamas.org/docs/data-ingestion/?
Contact information ddh-management@sub.uni-goettingen.de

MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS

Element Description
Editorial The main editorial requirements coincide with the DDH six Diamond criteria:

- Persistent identification: having a registered ISSN for the journal

- Scholarly journal: having established a peer review process

- Open Access with open licences: providing the outputs in open access with an open license

- No fees: no fees of any kind are required from the author or the reader)

- Open to all authors: no specific affiliation of the author is required to publish

- Community-owned: the journal must be owned by public or not-for-profit organisations (or parts thereof) whose mission includes performing or promoting research and scholarship.
Data file format None
Metadata standard The standard used by the DDH is the Journal Metadata Exchange Format (JMEF).

It is fully described as an XML schema here: https://github.com/CeON/jmef.

Its content is also available here: https://ddh.diamas.org/docs/ddh-metadata/.

Other requirements To be recorded in the EDCH Registry, and therefore to be eligible as a DDH Trusted source, it is mandatory to respect the following criteria:

- Be active in the Diamond OA ecosystem: Operate as a Publisher, Service Provider, or Tools and Technology Provider supporting Diamond OA publishing.

- Commit to community ownership: Demonstrate governance by and for the community, including researchers and scholars.

- Be a public legal entity: Have a defined legal status (non-profit, academic institution, government entity, or for-profit with Diamond OA support) to ensure transparency.

- Be part of the European landscape: Must be either located in - or have a parent organisation located in - a country that is part of the European Research Area (ERA) or an observer to the European Research Area and Innovation Committee (ERAC).
